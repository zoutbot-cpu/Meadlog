Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

Mead Log is a personal mead-brewing logbook. This page explains what data the app touches and how it's used.

What data the app accesses

Mead Log stores the batch information you enter yourself: recipe names, gravity readings, ingredients, and notes. This data is:

  • Saved in your browser's local storage by default, on the device you're using.
  • Optionally synced to your own Google Drive, if you choose to click "Connect Drive."

Google Drive access

If you connect Google Drive, Mead Log requests the drive.appdata scope only. This is a narrow, Google-defined permission that lets an app read and write a single hidden folder tied specifically to that app — it does not grant access to any other files in your Drive, and nothing you store elsewhere in your Drive is visible to this app.

The only thing written to that folder is your own mead-batch data, as a single JSON file, so it can follow you across devices and browsers.

What we don't do

  • We don't share, sell, or transmit your data to any third party.
  • We don't use analytics or tracking scripts.
  • We don't run ads.
  • We don't access, read, or store any other files in your Google Drive.

Revoking access

You can disconnect Google Drive access at any time from your Google Account permissions page. This immediately revokes Mead Log's access; your data stays in the app-specific Drive folder unless you also delete it there, or continues to live locally in your browser regardless.

Local data

Clearing your browser's site data/local storage for this page will remove any batches not also synced to Drive.

Contact

Questions or concerns about this app can be raised via the GitHub repository's issues page.
